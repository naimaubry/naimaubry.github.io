Current Research

Development and Evaluation of a Hybrid Turbulence Model for Improved Nuclear CFD Predictions

Conducting computational fluid dynamics research focused on turbulence modeling for nuclear applications under Emilio Baglietto, implementing and benchmarking LES, k–ε, k–ω, Spalart–Allmaras, and STRUCT models on separated-flow cases, such as the periodic hills test case. Work includes deriving and coding additional transport terms, using OpenFOAM to run simulations and develop scripts, and ensure numerical stability while comparing production, dissipation, and nonlocal effects across models. Building toward development of a hybrid turbulence model, with validation on benchmark cases and potential publication of results.
